This Is For You If…

  • You keep promising yourself you’ll stop, then end up back in the same cycle.
  • You’ve done detox, treatment, meetings, or counselling before — but still struggle with consistency after.
  • You don’t just need motivation. You need structure, accountability, and someone who will tell you the truth without shaming you.
  • You’re trying to rebuild trust with your family, your work, your health, and yourself.
  • You want recovery to become a lifestyle, not just a streak of clean days.

Why I Do This Work

I work with people who are tired of being told to “just stop” when the real problem is that their whole life has been built around surviving, avoiding, escaping, or numbing.

My approach is direct, compassionate, and structured. I believe people change when they have honesty, accountability, support, and a realistic plan they can actually follow.

This work is not about judging your past. It is about helping you build a life that no longer requires you to keep running from yourself.

FAQ

Do I need to be completely sober to work with you?

No. You do not need to have everything figured out before reaching out. If you are looking for substance use counselling in BC, the consultation is a place to talk through what is happening and whether this level of support fits where you are right now.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes. Sessions are primarily virtual, with in-person options depending on location and availability. Virtual addiction counselling can work well when you want steady support without adding extra travel or privacy concerns.

Do you work with people in Vancouver and the Lower Mainland?

Yes. I’m based in Vancouver and work with clients across the Lower Mainland and BC. If you’re looking for addiction counselling or recovery coaching in this area, the free consultation is a good place to talk through what kind of support fits.

Is this therapy?

No. This service provides addiction counselling and recovery coaching, including practical relapse prevention support and recovery accountability coaching for day-to-day follow-through. It does not include diagnosis, psychotherapy, psychiatric care, or emergency crisis services.

Can family members book a consultation?

Yes. Family members can reach out to discuss support, boundaries, communication, and next steps. This kind of family addiction support can help loved ones understand the recovery process without enabling destructive behaviour.

What if I’m in crisis?

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department. This service is not a crisis or emergency service.
